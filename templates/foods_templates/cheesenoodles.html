In Munich: Käsespätzle

Continuing my culinary journey through Munich, Germany, I have to tell you all about a dish that surprised and delighted me: Käsespätzle. Now, the name might sound a little intimidating, but trust me, this dish is pure comfort food heaven.
Imagine soft, pillowy egg noodles, tossed in a creamy, melted cheese sauce. That's Käsespätzle in a nutshell. The spätzle themselves were a revelation. Unlike any pasta I'd ever had, they were small, irregular dumplings with a delightful chewiness. They provided the perfect base for the rich and decadent cheese sauce.

The specific Käsespätzle I had at a charming little restaurant called Zum Schneider was pure magic. They used a combination of cheeses, including Emmentaler and Gruyere, which created a complex and satisfying flavor profile. The cheese wasn't overpowering, but it clung perfectly to the spätzle, coating them in a blanket of cheesy goodness.

The beauty of Käsespätzle is its simplicity. This dish doesn't need a lot of fanfare. At Zum Schneider, it came with a side of caramelized onions, which added a touch of sweetness and a delightful savory contrast. It was the perfect accompaniment, but honestly, the spätzle and cheese were so good on their own, they could have stood alone.
Of course, no German meal is complete without a good beer, and this cheesy delight was no exception. I opted for a local helles lager, which was light and crisp, perfectly cutting through the richness of the cheese sauce. It was a match made in Bavarian heaven.

This wasn't just a plate of food; it was a warm hug on a cool Munich evening. The cozy atmosphere of Zum Schneider, the friendly service, and that incredible Käsespätzle – it was an experience I won't soon forget.
So, if you're ever in Munich with a hankering for something cheesy and comforting, put Käsespätzle on your list. And if you find yourself at Zum Schneider, you're in for a real treat. This dish is a cheesy masterpiece, and a memory I'll be savoring until my next German adventure.

Käsespätzle (German Cheese Spaetzle)

Serve: 6

Prep Time:30 Minutes

Cooking Time: 30 Minutes

Rating: 5.0/5 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (1 voted)

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 batch Homemade German Spätzle (about 5 cups cooked Spätzle, can use store-bought if preferred), can be made in advance and refrigerated until ready use, then let warm to room temperature before baking.
  • 6 tablespoons butter
  • 2-3 large onions , chopped (the more caramelized onions the better so I go with at least 3!)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 12 ounces shredded Emmentaler or Jarlsberg (Swiss-like from Norway, mild) (you can also add some Gruyere for extra flavor)
  • Salt

RECIPIES

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F. Butter a 9x13 (or a little smaller) casserole dish.
  2. To make the caramelized onions: Melt the butter in a medium-sized heavy stock pot or Dutch oven. Add the onions and stir occasionally for 20-30 minutes until deeply caramelized. Halfway into it sprinkle with a little salt and sugar to help with the caramelizing. Get the onions nice and brown.
  3. Layer 1/3 of the Spätzle in the bottom of the dish followed by 1/3 of the cheese and 1/3 of the caramelized onions. Repeat, sprinkling each layer with some salt, ending with cheese and onions on top.
  4. Bake for 10 minutes or longer until the cheese is melted and the edges are just beginning to get a little crispy.
  5. Serve immediately. This makes great leftovers!
