Paradise with a Punch: Pork Sinigang at Coco Palms Resort

Hey everyone, Jim Ryan here, checking in live from the stunning Coco Palms Resort! Sun-kissed skin, crystal clear waters, and the gentle swaying of palm trees – it's pure bliss. But let's be honest, no vacation is complete without some incredible food, and this place is definitely delivering.
Today's star? Their legendary Pork Sinigang. Now, I'm a self-proclaimed sinigang aficionado. I've had this beloved Filipino sour soup countless times, but the one here takes the cake – or should I say, the mangoes?

The first thing that hit me was the aroma. As the bowl arrived, a wave of lemongrass, garlic, and a delightful tang wafted up, instantly whetting my appetite. The broth itself was a symphony of flavors. The tamarind base provided the perfect sour punch, balanced beautifully by the sweetness of tomatoes and a hint of fish sauce.

But what truly elevated this dish was the pork. Tender, melt-in-your-mouth chunks of pork belly swam in the vibrant broth, each bite bursting with savory goodness. The vegetables were cooked to perfection – crisp okra, green beans with a slight snap, and plump eggplant adding wonderful textural contrast.
Now, I wouldn't be doing this sinigang justice if I didn't mention the accompaniments. A generous portion of fluffy white rice was the perfect partner to soak up all that delicious broth. A side of bagoong (shrimp paste) on the table offered an optional salty kick for those who crave a bit more oomph.

Let me tell you, folks, this wasn't just a meal; it was an experience. Sitting on the open-air veranda, the sound of the ocean waves in the background, and this incredible pork sinigang in front of me – it was pure relaxation in a bowl. It was the perfect balance of refreshing, comforting, and bursting with flavor.
If you ever find yourself at Coco Palms Resort, do yourself a favor and order their Pork Sinigang. You won't regret it. Trust me, it's a taste of paradise with a delightful punch!

Pork Sinigang

Serve: 4

Prep Time:15 Minutes

Cooking Time:1 Hour

Rating: 5.0/5 ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (1 voted)

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 pounds pork spare ribs, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 large tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 medium onion, peeled and quartered
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 6 pieces gabi, (peeled and halved depending on size)
  • 1 6-inch radish (labanos), peeled and sliced to ½-inch thick half-rounds
  • 2 finger chilies (siling haba)
  • ½ bunch long beans (sitaw), ends trimmed and cut into 3-inch lengths
  • 1 eggplant, ends trimmed and sliced to ½-inch thick half-rounds
  • 6 pieces okra, ends trimmed
  • 15 pieces large tamarind or 1 ½ (1.41 ounces each) packages tamarind base powder
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 bunch bok choy or pechay, ends trimmed and separated into leaves

RECIPIES

  1. Rinse pork ribs and drain well.
  2. In a pot over medium heat, combine pork and enough water to cover. Bring to a boil, skimming scum that accumulates on top.
  3. Once broth clears, add tomatoes, onion, and fish sauce. Lower heat and simmer for about 1 to 1 ½ hours or until meat is tender, adding more water as necessary to maintain about 8 cups.
  4. Add gabi and cook for about 4 to 6 minutes or until tender.
  5. Add chili peppers and radish. Continue to simmer for about 2 to 3 minutes.
  6. Add long beans. Continue to cook for about 2 minutes.
  7. Add eggplant and okra and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes.
  8. If using packaged tamarind base, add to the pot and stir until completely dissolved.
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Add bok choy and continue to cook for about 1 minute. Serve hot.
